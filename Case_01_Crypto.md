# Case Study 02: Fund Diversion via Crypto-Assets (KYC Inconsistency)

**Typology:** Use of cryptocurrency exchanges for fund dispersion (*Layering*) and inconsistency in client profile.
**Trigger/Alert:** A medium-risk client executes repetitive, massive transfers to a Cryptocurrency Exchange immediately after receiving government subsidies/grants.

---

## 1. Criminological & Operational Analysis
**Subject:** Ana Martínez (Fictitious name), registered as "unemployed" in the Know Your Customer (KYC) onboarding form.
* **Detected Anomaly:** The account receives a €15,000 inbound wire transfer under the concept "Employment Grant." Within less than 2 hours, 95% of the funds are layered out across 4 split transactions to a Crypto Exchange (Binance).
* **Risk Indicator:** The subject's profile as an unemployed individual does not justify the financial capacity for immediate high-volume investment. Furthermore, there is a lack of economic logic in routing public welfare funds into speculative or potentially obfuscating digital assets.

---

## 2. Official STR/SAR Narrative (Financial Intelligence Unit Format)

**EXECUTIVE SUMMARY:** This Suspicious Transaction Report (STR) is generated following the detection of immediate fund dispersion and transactional behavior inconsistent with the client's socio-economic profile.

**INVESTIGATION DETAILS:** The operational analyst reviewed the KYC file of the subject, who is currently registered as unemployed with no declared steady income. On June 15, the account received a €15,000 government grant. Within two hours of consolidation, the subject ordered 4 consecutive transfers to a cryptocurrency exchange platform, effectively depleting the account balance. 

**CONCLUSION & RECOMMENDATION:** The analyst identified a total disconnect between the subject's socio-labor profile, the nature of the funds (welfare aid), and their final destination (crypto-assets). The velocity of the transactions (*rapid movement of funds*) strongly suggests an attempt to obfuscate the money trail (*layering*) to evade state regulatory oversight or potential asset freezing. 

Based on these criminological and transactional indicators, there is a well-founded suspicion of fraud and potential money laundering. The analyst recommends filing this STR to the Financial Intelligence Unit (FIU) and escalating the case to the Risk Committee for account closure.
